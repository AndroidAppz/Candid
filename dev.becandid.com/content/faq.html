Frequently Asked Questions

What is Candid?

Candid is a social platform that personalizes your news feed and lets you discuss your favorite topics anonymously with friends and strangers.

Is Candid safe?

Yes! Candid is totally safe. No one will ever know your identity.

Candid doesn't know your identity and cannot leak it.

When you upload personal information to the service, as little as possible is stored, and all data is encrypted via a one-way hash, which means we have no way of reading it.

If Candid is private, why do you need my Facebook login?

Candid uses a unique one-way encryption to make sure that no one can know your identity.

If you link your Facebook to Candid, we only use the link to (anonymously) highlight posts from friends.

What can I share on Candid?

Candid is especially great for letting people know about a secret you've been wanting to share but don't want people to know came from you. It's also great to share your opinions about culture, sports, politics, or anything that you want to have a real conversation about, but don't want coming back to bite you.

Can I report posts or comments that shouldn't be on Candid?

Yes! Please report all posts that shouldn't be on Candid by pressing the downward-facing triangle next to the username, selecting "Report This Post," and choosing the appropriate reason.

Candid's AI is improving all the time, and your reports help keep the service safe.

If you'd prefer, you can also select "Don't Show Me This" and that post and its comments will be hidden.

What should I do if I think I may harm myself?

We take any threats of self-harm or suicide seriously. If you are worried that you may harm yourself and live in the United States, please call the National Suicide Prevention Line: 1 (800) 273-8255.

They are available 24 hours a day.

If you are outside the United States, please find the suicide hotlines for your country here.

You can also visit: suicidepreventionlifeline.org

Why does Candid use Kochava?

In order to attribute installs from different sources (such as downloads from Twitter ads for Candid), we use Kochava, solely for the purpose of tracking application installs. This data is not stored on our servers, or correlated in any way with user data or content.
